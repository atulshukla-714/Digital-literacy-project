# Task 5 – Cybercrime Awareness Case Study

## Title: UPI Scam Through Fake Customer Support

Rohit, a college student, wanted help regarding a failed online order. He searched for the customer care number on Google and unknowingly called a fake number posted by scammers. The fraudster pretended to be a support executive and gained Rohit’s trust by speaking professionally.

The scammer then asked Rohit to install a screen-sharing app and instructed him to open his UPI app. Step-by-step, the fraudster guided him to “verify” his account, but actually tricked him into approving a payment request. Rohit unknowingly entered his UPI PIN, believing it was for receiving a refund. Instead, ₹15,000 was deducted from his account.

Such scams usually target students, elderly people, and those unfamiliar with digital payments. The criminals exploit urgency, confusion, and trust to manipulate victims.

The consequences include financial loss, emotional stress, and loss of confidence in online systems. In many cases, victims are unable to recover their money if they delay reporting.

This case highlights the importance of verifying sources, never sharing sensitive information, and understanding that entering a UPI PIN always means sending money, not receiving it.
